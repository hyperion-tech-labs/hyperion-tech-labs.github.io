The Problem

New Reality, a B2B virtual reality (VR) company, was looking to design and build a new method for assessing an individual's level of skill at a particular task in a VR environment. This would enable them to give faster feedback and increase the effectiveness and speed of the learning process for any particular set of tasks one wishes to train someone on. Such a system would be a new analytics engine that is able to form the foundation of assessing and improving skills in the workplace. New Reality would use the solution to "digitize" current traditional training in the market bringing a disruptive shift to this South African sector through effective analytics.

The Solution

Hyperion Labs proposed a speed reading application that uses a perceptual expansion method to train the user. The speed reading application was chosen as any test participant would not require prior training to obtain a control score. After a user undergoes the VR training procedure a second measurement is taken in order to determine the percentage improvement in the users reading speed. This method allows New Reality to quantitatively prove that a skill may be trained on a virtual reality platform.

The process of constructing an MVP is important as the data that is measured and analyzed must be piped to the correct databases and cloud compute platforms. Ensuring smooth flow of data is an essential foundation to building any data sets for machine learning applications. The diagram below outlines the Hyperion Labs solution.

New Reality uses the MVP application in order to gain further insights into the prospects of learning in fully immersive environments. Further iterations of the application is used as a demo for potential clients to experience the power of accelerated learning within virtual reality environments. The Hyperion Labs solution has paved the way for New Reality to build an entirely new revenue stream through a revolutionary product for the training and education sector.

Result

On average, users of the speed reading app displayed a 200% increase in reading speed after a single round of training. This indicates that a skill can be taught and users quantitatively display an improvement in performing the task taught to them. Hyperion Labs proved that value can be added to a company’s strategy and technological capability through an innovation partnership.
